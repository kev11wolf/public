The Exact Cut Sheet

Measure twice, cut once with a hand saw or circular saw. Label each piece with a pencil!

  • From 2x6 Board #1: Cut into two pieces exactly 48 inches long. (Label: Outer Front Rim and Outer Back Rim).
  • From 2x6 Board #2: Cut into two pieces exactly 45 inches long. (Label: Outer Left Rim and Outer Right Rim).
  • From 2x6 Board #3: Cut into two pieces exactly 45 inches long. (Label: Inner Joist A and Inner Joist B).
  • From the seven 2x4 Boards: Cut every single board exactly in half to create fourteen 48-inch long boards. (Label: Floor Slats. You will use 13, keep 1 as a spare).

Noob-Proof Assembly Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ground Corners: Place your 4 deck blocks roughly in a 4'x4' square on your lawn slope. Use a shovel to dig out a shallow, flat pocket in the grass *only under the uphill blocks* until all 4 blocks sit visually level with each other. Pour 2 inches of gravel into the dug-out pockets to stop the blocks from settling into wet clay soil.
  2. Build the Outer Box Frame: Lay out your 2x6 boards on a flat surface. Stand them up on edge. Place your two 45" Outer Left/Right boards inside the ends of your two 48" Outer Front/Back boards to make a perfect square.

    Fastening Step:

    Pre-drilling is REQUIRED. Use a 1/8" drill bit to drill 3 holes through the face of the 48" board where it overlaps the end of the 45" board. Drive three certified 3-1/2" GRK R4 Heavy-Duty Structural Wood Screws (T-25 Star Drive) through those holes into the end of the 45" board. Repeat at all four corners. Do not use standard deck screws here or the corners will snap apart under load.
  3. Drop Frame Into Blocks: Lift your completed 48"x48" square frame and drop its bottom edge into the slots on top of the 4 deck blocks. Place a 4-foot bubble level on top of the frame. If it's tilted, lift the frame out and adjust the gravel/dirt under the low blocks until the bubble sits perfectly in the center lines.
  4. Install Inner Support Joists: Inside your box frame, mark lines on the *Outer Front* and *Outer Back* boards at exactly 16 inches and 32 inches from the left corner. Hold your 2x6 metal joist hangers centered on these marks and flush with the bottom of the frame.

    Fastening Step:

    No pre-drilling needed for hangers. Use your drill to drive six 1-1/2" Simpson Strong-Tie SD9112 Structural Connector Screws through the face holes of the metal hanger directly into the frame box. Drop your two 45" Inner Joists into the hangers. Drive four more 1-1/2" screws through the side tabs of the hanger directly into the joist wood. **Never** use drywall or deck screws in metal hangers.
  5. Attach the Floor Slat Face: Lay your 48" long 2x4 floor boards flat across the top of the frame, starting from one edge. Place a carpenter's pencil between each board to maintain a uniform 1/4" drainage gap.

    Fastening Step:

    Pre-drilling is only required within 2 inches of the ends of the boards to prevent splitting. Use a 1/8" drill bit. Drive two 3" Premium Coated Exterior Wood Deck Screws down through the flat face of the 2x4 directly into the 2x6 frame/joist underneath at every spot they cross.

The Exact Cut Sheet

  • 4x4 Support Posts: Cut all 4 posts to exactly 7 feet long. (4 feet provides the height from ground to floor, and the remaining 3 feet extends above the floor to form the safety guardrail posts).
  • 2x6 Framing Lumber: Same cuts as the low platform (Two at 48" for front/back rims, four at 45" for side rims and inner joists).
  • 2x4 Surface Floor Slats: Cut 7 boards in half to make fourteen 48" lengths (use 13).
  • 2x4 Guardrails: Cut 3 boards in half to make six 48" planks (Label: Safety Rails).
  • 2x4 Diagonal Braces: 4 remaining full 8' boards will be cut-to-fit on-site during installation.

Noob-Proof Assembly Instructions

  1. Level & Pin the Blocks: Dig flat shelves into your grass slope at each corner of a 4'x4' square. Add gravel, and place the 4 deck blocks. Drive a **24" Steel Rebar Stake** completely into the dirt directly down the downhill edge of each block with a sledgehammer. This blocks the concrete bases from sliding down the slope under force.
  2. Stand Up the 4x4 Posts: Insert your 7-foot 4x4 posts vertically into the center square pockets of the deck blocks. Have a helper hold a level against the side of each post to make sure it stands perfectly straight up (plumb).
  3. Build & Mount the Frame: Assemble the outer 48"x48" 2x6 frame box on the ground exactly like Step 2 of the Low Platform using 3-1/2" GRK R4 Heavy-Duty Structural Wood Screws. Slide the completed wood frame box *around* the outside of your four standing posts. Lift the frame up until its top edge is exactly 48 inches off the ground. Clamp it tightly to the posts.

    Fastening Step:

    Pre-drilling is **STRICTLY MANDATORY** here to prevent splitting the heavy vertical timbers. Use a 3/16" drill bit to drill 2 diagonal pilot holes through the 2x6 frame and into the center of the 4x4 post at each corner. Drive two heavy-duty 5" Simpson Strong-Tie SDWS Framing Structural Wood Screws (T-30 Star Drive) through the frame into each post.
  4. Joists & Floor Slats: Install your 2x6 inner joists using 1-1/2" Simpson Strong-Tie SD9112 Structural Connector Screws in your hangers. Lay down your 2x4 floor slats using 3" Premium Coated Exterior Wood Deck Screws exactly as outlined in the low platform guide.
  5. Install Lower Diagonal X-Braces: Hold a full 2x4 board diagonally across two lower 4x4 posts (underneath the platform frame). Use a pencil to trace the angle where it meets the posts, cut along the lines, and place it back on the posts to form a diagonal brace. Repeat on three open sides.

    Fastening Step:

    Pre-drill through the 2x4 brace using a 3/16" drill bit. Secure each end of the brace to the 4x4 post by driving two heavy-duty 3-1/2" Simpson Strong-Tie SDWS Structural Wood Screws (T-30 Star Drive) tightly into the posts.
  6. Install the Guardrails: Take your 48" long 2x4 safety rail planks. Mount them horizontally across the upper 3-foot section of the 4x4 posts. Mount one row at 18 inches above the floor, and the top row flush with the top of the posts at 36 inches.

    Fastening Step:

    Pre-drill the ends with a 1/8" bit. Secure each rail to the post face using two 3" Premium Coated Exterior Wood Deck Screws at each end connection point.

Repeat this down the line until the patio platform is covered.

    Fastening Step:

    Pre-drilling through the 2x4 timber is required to guarantee straight trajectory into the studs. Use a 1/8" drill bit. At every single stud mark location, drive **TWO** vertically stacked 3-1/2" GRK R4 Heavy-Duty Structural Wood Screws (T-25 Star Drive) straight through the 2x4 cleat and drywall directly into the center of the studs. This locks the shelf into the structural bones of your home.
  2. Assemble the Heavy Internal Ladder on a Workbench: Do not try to build this piece-by-piece on the wall. Lay out your outer 2x4 shelf frame on a flat table. Stand your 13" *Ladder Ribs* up on edge inside the frame, spaced precisely **12 inches On-Center (O.C.)**.

    Fastening Step:

    No pre-drilling required for the frame wood-to-wood joints. Secure the ribs to the outer cap frame by driving two 3" GRK R4 Multi-Purpose Structural Wood Screws through the exterior face of the frame directly into the end grains of each support rib. This creates an unyielding truss matrix.
  3. Sleeve and Interlock the Framework: Slide your pre-assembled workbench ladder frame directly over your wall-mounted 2x4 cleats. It should fit snugly like a sleeve over a block. Clamp the elements tightly together to compress any wall gaps.

    Fastening Step:

    Drive 3" GRK R4 Multi-Purpose Structural Wood Screws vertically straight down through the top face of the internal ladder frame into the top edge of the wall cleats below every 8 inches across the entire perimeter path. This interlocks the cantilevered frame and removes all hinge drop-play.
  4. Skin the Frame & Seal for Water Disasters: Run a continuous line of waterproof wood glue across the top edges of all 2x4 frame joists. Lay your 3/4" Birch plywood top skin flat over the assembly.

    Fastening Step:

    Use a countersink bit to drill shallow pilot holes so screw heads don't stick up and wobble your plant pots. Secure the plywood down using 1-5/8" Premium Trim-Head finishing screws spaced every 6 inches along all joist lines. Repeat on the bottom face with the lower skin panel. Glue and trim the front face with your 1x6 clear trim apron board.
  5. Apply the Plant-Proof Longevity Shielding: Sand all wood faces smooth. Vacuum up all sawdust. Brush on **three thick continuous coats of Minwax Helmsman Marine Spar Urethane** across the entire shelf surface, allowing 4 hours of dry-time between coats. Once dry, run a bead of clear **OSI QUAD MAX caulk** right along the back seam where the shelf plywood meets your wall drywall. This stops water spills from traveling behind your shelf structure and inducing mold growth.

Playground Accessories Installation Blueprint

Safety Core Rule: All playground accessories must be fastened directly into the thick, load-bearing 2x6 structural frame or 4x4 posts. **Never** fasten playground accessories solely into the thin 2x4 floor boards, as they will pull loose and fail.

1. Solid Rock Climbing Wall

Best attached to an open side of your 4-foot high elevated platform.

How to Build and Attach:
  1. Take a sheet of 3/4" exterior-grade plywood or line up 2x6 boards side-by-side to form a 4-foot wide by 5-foot long panel.
  2. Drill 1/2" holes randomly across the face of the wood. Hammer metal playground T-nuts tightly into those holes from the **backside** of the wood. Screw your climbing holds onto the front face into those T-nuts.
  3. Lean the climbing wall against your platform frame at a comfortable angle.

    4. Fastening Step:

    Pre-drill through the top plywood lip using a 1/8" bit. Drive five heavy 3-1/2" GRK R4 Heavy-Duty Structural Wood Screws through the top edge of the climbing wall panel directly into the platform's main 2x6 outer frame. For an advanced connection, use heavy-duty galvanized gate hinges bolted through the frame with nuts and washers.

2. Heavy-Duty Climbing Rope Ladder

Hangs vertically down from the edge of the 4-foot high platform frame.

How to Build and Attach:
  1. Purchase pre-assembled climbing ropes or thread 3/4" thick natural manila rope through holes drilled into the ends of 2x2 wood rungs spaced 12 inches apart. Tie large knots directly beneath each rung.

    2. Fastening Step:

    Go underneath the 4-foot platform floor. Take two **1/2" Heavy-Duty Galvanized Screw Eye Bolts**. Pre-drill holes into the bottom edge of the platform's 2x6 structural frame with a 3/8" drill bit. Twist the eye bolts completely into the 2x6 frame until the threads disappear. Loop your ropes through these steel rings and secure them with climbing-grade triple knots or locking carabiners.

3. Static Walkway Bridge vs. Tensioned Suspension Bridge

How to construct a bridge link between your 12-inch low platform and your 4-foot high platform.

Option A: The Rigid Static Walkway (Easier & Unmoving)

  1. Cut two 2x6 beams to the span distance between the two structures. Place them bridging the gap.

    2. Fastening Step:

    Pre-drill with a 3/16" bit. Secure the ends of these bridge beams directly to the faces of the 4x4 posts or 2x6 platform rims using two thick 5/16" x 5" Simpson Strong-Tie SDWS Structural Wood Screws (T-30 drive) per joint. Screw 2x4 flat floor steps across the beams using 3" Premium Coated Exterior Wood Deck Screws.

Option B: The Swinging Suspension Bridge (Advanced Challenge)

  1. Purchase two lengths of 3/8" thick galvanized proof-coil steel chains to bridge the distance.

    2. Fastening Step:

    Drill completely through the 4x4 posts on both platforms using a 1/2" long drill bit. Install threaded **1/2" Galvanized Thimble Eye Bolts** through the holes, securing them tightly with washers and nuts on the opposite side with a wrench. Hook the chains to these loops using heavy-duty locking threaded chain links. Secure 2x4 wooden foot-steps across the chains by clamping them from underneath using steel U-bolts wrapped around the links.

4. Toy Pulley System with Cargo Bucket

Mounts out from a 4x4 corner guardrail post on the elevated platform.

How to Build and Attach:
  1. Take a 2-foot long 2x4 scrap piece to act as a crane arm. Screw it horizontally to the top of a 4x4 post so it sticks out over the yard edge. Add a small diagonal 2x4 wedge underneath it to act as a supporting arm.

    2. Fastening Step:

    Pre-drill the connection layout with a 1/8" bit. Secure the wood bracket to the post face using four structural 3-1/2" GRK R4 Heavy-Duty Structural Wood Screws (T-25 Drive). Screw a heavy steel hook into the end of the overhanging arm. Hang a **4-inch Fixed Swivel Pulley Wheel** from the hook. Thread a 3/8" nylon rope through the wheel and tie one end to a steel canvas bucket handle.

Beginner Foundations & Tool Settings Guide

Understanding Pre-Drilling and Screws

  • Why Pre-Drill? Driving large screws directly into thick pressure-treated wood forces the wood fibers apart, causing out-of-sight splitting that compromises the safety of your frame. Pre-drilling removes a small core of wood first, allowing the screw threads to cut smoothly and cleanly into the material.
  • Bit Sizes Rule of Thumb: Your drill bit should be the same size as the solid inner center shank of the screw, *not* the extended width of the outer threads.
    • For 3" deck screws: Use a 1/8" bit.
    • For heavy structural framing screws (GRK R4 / Spax): Use a 1/8" or 5/32" bit.
    • For thick 5/16" structural lag screws (Simpson SDWS): Use a 3/16" bit.
  • The Crown Rule: Look down the narrow edge of every board before cutting or installing. It will bow slightly in one direction. Always install the board with that curve arching **upward** like a rainbow. Over time, physical weight and gravity will flatten the board out. If you install it upside down, it will permanently sag and pool rainwater.

Raleigh, NC Longevity Shielding

  • Combating Wet Clay Soil: Central North Carolina clay dries out very slowly. Concrete deck blocks easily pull moisture out of the earth and transfer it directly into your wood frames. To prevent rotting at contact points, apply a layer of adhesive **butyl joist tape** or heavy waterproof flashing tape along the bottom edge of any wood framing that rests inside the block slots.
  • The Lumber Dry-Out Period: Brand new pressure-treated lumber from local big-box stores is injected with heavy liquid chemical preservatives and is often soaking wet. **Do not seal or paint the wood immediately.** Let your completed structures sit unsealed in the yard for 4 to 6 weeks until the wood feels bone dry to the touch. Once acclimated, apply a premium exterior water-repellent stain.
  • Annual Inspections: Active play causes vibrations that can gradually back screws out of their threads. Re-tighten all structural fasteners every spring to ensure your structure stays rigid and safe.

Raleigh Front Screen Porch Project (Mini Track System)

Project Scope: Screening a covered front porch under roof with two 6x8 openings, two 10x8 openings, and one 8x8 stair opening. Tying directly into existing painted 4x4 columns sitting on brick crowns without structural modifications.
Core Wind-Load Strategy: Heavy 10-foot wide spans are divided into dual 5x8 screen fields using a vertical 2x4 mid-post. This prevents mesh sag, resists wind pull, and maximizes architectural symmetry.
⚠️ Raleigh, NC Code Compliance Checklist:
  • Guardrail Mandate: If your porch floor sits higher than 30 inches off adjacent ground grade, an interior guardrail is legally required. It must stand at least 36" high with baluster spacing preventing a 4" sphere from passing through.
  • Stair Landing Door Law: Per NC Residential Building Code, screen doors cannot swing outward over steps without an exterior landing at least 36" deep. Because the stairs drop immediately, this screen door frame layout is engineered to swing inward toward the porch footprint only.
  • Brick Protection Principle: To preserve historic or painted brick piers, zero fasteners drill into masonry crowns. Framed inserts wedge securely and anchor only into structural wood columns and overhead roof beams.

The Master Project Cut Sheet

Note: Always measure individual openings before cutting. Older front porches naturally drop up to 1" across long spans for water drainage.

  • 10x8 Spans (Mid-Post Organizers): Cut 2 pieces of 2x4 to exactly 96" (or precise floor-to-beam clearance height). Face-mount flatwise.
  • Stair Framing Top Header Plate: Cut 1 piece of 2x4 to exactly 96". Mounts flatwise to ceiling beam.
  • Stair Frame Vertical King Studs: Cut 2 pieces of 2x4 to 92.5" (Compensates for top plate layout thickness).
  • Stair Frame Door Rough Header: Cut 1 piece of 2x4 to exactly 36-1/4".
  • Stair Framing Floor Plate (Side Screen Slat Base): Cut 1 piece of 2x4 to 59-3/4". Stops cleanly at door opening pathway.
  • Independent Structural 4x4 Inner Rail Posts: Cut 6 posts to exactly 44" long (Yields 36.5" rail height + 7.5" drop length for internal rim cavity joist-bolting).
  • 6x8 Span Interior Guardrails: Cut 4 pieces of 2x4 down to 72" (Trim on-site between inner 4x4 posts).
  • 10x8 Span Interior Guardrails: Cut 4 pieces of 2x4 down to 120" (Trim on-site between inner 4x4 posts).
  • Stair Span Interior Guardrails: Cut 2 pieces of 2x4 down to 60" (Trim to fit).

Step-by-Step Execution Guide

  1. Erect Mid-Posts & Stair Frame: Find the exact center points of the two 10-foot wide column openings. Place an 8-ft 2x4 vertical mid-post flat-wise (broad 3.5" face outward) inline with the columns. Secure to upper beam and wood floor using pocket holes on the interior side. Move to the stair opening: fasten the 96" top plate to the header beam. Anchor vertical king studs down to the deck floor exactly 36-1/4" apart. Screw the door header across them at 80-1/2" from the deck floor. This forms a clean 36-1/4" x 80-1/2" rough opening for the door and an upper 15.5" transom screen box.

    Fastening Step:

    Use a Kreg Pocket Hole jig on the interior sides of all new 2x4 framing elements. Drive two weather-proof 2-1/2" Kreg Blue-Kote Outdoor Pocket Hole Screws per structural connection point. Never drill downward into the historic brick or decorative crown trim blocks.
  2. Independent Interior Guardrail Build: Drop your 44" long 4x4 posts inside the porch area, sitting flush against the inner face of the perimeter floor rim joist. Space posts evenly behind your screen sections. Mount lower 2x4 horizontal rails 3.5" above the deck floor. Secure top 2x4 rails flat at the 36" mark. Place balusters 4.5" on-center across the tracks to maintain code safety.

    Fastening Step:

    Secure each 4x4 inner post to the framing perimeter rim joists by drilling a clean 1/2" pilot hole and driving two heavy-duty 6-1/4" FastenMaster ThruLOK Structural Rim Screws directly through the post base and floor frame perimeter. This creates a fully legal safety barrier completely decoupled from the external screen fabric lines.
  3. Prep, Paint, and Caulk: Fill all exterior pocket screw holes with wood plugs or specialized exterior filler. Sand smooth. Run a bead of advanced exterior caulk where the new 2x4 framing components meet the existing painted 4x4 porch columns. Prime and paint the framing structure to blend cleanly with your existing house column paint.
  4. Mount Mini Track Clips and Track: Place Mini Track clips around the exterior faces of every single window section layout. Secure clips exactly 2 inches away from every corner, and space them every 8 to 10 inches along all vertical and horizontal runs using the specialized stainless steel screws included in the clip package. Once all clips are screwed down, use a miter trim snip to cut your aluminum tracking to length and snap them firmly over the clips until they click.
  5. Stretch Screen Mesh & Spline: Cut a panel of screen mesh 6 inches wider and taller than the target field opening. Use painter's tape to tack it flush across the upper track. Run your spline tool along the top track channel, pressing the 0.170" spline over the mesh. Pull the screen downward smoothly and roll into the bottom track, then finish the left and right sides.

    Fastening Step:

    Hold a sharp utility knife flat against the exterior perimeter edge of the aluminum track frame. Trim away excess mesh with a single continuous stroke. Repeat panel-by-panel for all split fields and the door transom window.
  6. Hang the Screen Door: Prime and paint your 5-bar natural wood screen door to match the frame. Mount the heavy-duty spring hinges inside the rough opening face so the door opens inward towards the porch deck. Attach the locking hardware latch handle assembly using the weather-rated screws provided in the gate kit, test the clearance, and adjust hinge spring tension until the door closes cleanly and latches securely automatically.
