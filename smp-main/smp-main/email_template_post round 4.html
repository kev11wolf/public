He Did It Again. Rory McIlroy, Back-to-Back Masters Champion, the First Since Tiger Woods.

 

Good evening, friends. What you witnessed today at Augusta National was history — and not the quiet kind. Rory McIlroy, who lost a six-shot lead on Saturday and looked every bit like a man who had written the cruelest chapter of his own story, went out Sunday and rewrote the ending. He is your 2026 Masters champion at 12-under par, winning by one stroke, and he is the first man to wear back-to-back green jackets since Tiger Woods did it in 2001 and 2002. Some things are worth the wait. Some things are worth the journey. And some men are simply built for this place, even when this place tries everything it has to break them.

It was not easy, and it was never supposed to be. McIlroy stumbled early — a double-bogey on the fourth hole after a three-putt, a bogey on the sixth, and suddenly the man who started the day tied for the lead was two shots behind Cameron Young. The crowd at Augusta went quiet in that particular way it does when something unthinkable is brewing. But Rory did not flinch. Birdies on seven and eight steadied the ship. Then came Amen Corner — that stretch of land that has devoured careers and defined champions — and McIlroy walked through it like he owned it. A birdie from seven feet on the 12th. A 350-yard drive and a brilliant putt on the 13th to go three clear with five holes to play. A bogey on 18 mattered not one bit. The green jacket was already his.

With this, his sixth major championship, Rory McIlroy joins Nick Faldo as the second-most prolific major winner among European golfers. He has two green jackets now. He has earned the right to call Augusta home.
