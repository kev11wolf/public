🎯  Par 3 Contest

The always-fun Par 3 Contest tees off Wednesday before the tournament and gives Augusta week its most relaxed, family-heavy moment. It’s equal parts skill, smiles, and superstition too — because nobody has ever won the Par 3 Contest and the Masters in the same year.

📸 Augusta Ace History

The first hole in one in Masters history was made by Ross Somerville during the second round of the inaugural 1934 tournament, back when the event was still called the Augusta National Invitation Tournament. He made the ace at the 16th hole, giving the Somerville name a permanent place in Augusta lore right from the beginning.

Somerville was a Canadian amateur, and his shot came in the very first Masters ever played — a perfect early reminder that Augusta has been producing memorable moments since day one.
