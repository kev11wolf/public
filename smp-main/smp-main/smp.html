      📰 News & Updates

      ⛳ Round 2 — Friday at Augusta National

      A tradition unlike any other — and this one now has a number attached to it. Rory McIlroy stands at 12 under par, the largest 36-hole lead in the 90-year history of the Masters Tournament. Six strokes. He closed Friday with four consecutive birdies and six in a seven-hole span. His 36-hole total of 132 is the third-lowest ever recorded at Augusta National. Whatever Jack Nicklaus said to him before the week began, it has clearly been received. The defending champion is not holding on. He is pulling away.

      Patrick Reed — the 2018 champion — and Sam Burns sit at 6 under par, separated from McIlroy by a gulf that will require something close to perfection to close. Tommy Fleetwood produced the round of the day on the morning wave: two eagles, one on the par-5 eighth and a second not long after, finishing at 5 under alongside Shane Lowry and Justin Rose. Tyrrell Hatton fired a 66 to reach 4 under, joining Cameron Young, Jason Day, Haotong Li, Kristoffer Reitan, and Wyndham Clark in a crowded tier that at least has a conversation to be had on Saturday. Ben Koepka sits at 3 under. Jordan Spieth, always a threat around these grounds, is at 1 over.

      The subplot of the afternoon belonged to Jon Rahm. The 2023 Masters champion entered Friday at 6 over — a position that looked terminal. He refused to go quietly. A birdie at the 16th moved him to exactly 4 over par, right on the cut number, and the Spaniard will play the weekend. His survival was the kind of quiet, grinding Friday that Augusta occasionally rewards. He is not in the conversation for Sunday, but he is in the building, and at Augusta that matters.

      Brian Harman provided one of the most remarkable scorecards of the day: he stood at 10 over through just four holes on Friday morning. He then proceeded to make seven birdies over the final fourteen holes to finish at exactly 4 over and survive the cut by the narrowest of margins. Augusta does not always offer redemption arcs. On Friday it offered Harman one, and he took every stroke of it.

      ✂️ The Cut — Notable Departures

      The defining moment of the cut came on the 18th hole. Bryson DeChambeau stood on the tee needing only a bogey to play the weekend. He walked off the green with a triple bogey. That is not a misprint. He needed a five. He made a seven. He will not play Saturday. Augusta has a vocabulary all its own for moments like this, and the 18th green wrote a sentence on Friday afternoon that will not be quickly forgotten.

      Robert MacIntyre also will not play the weekend, nor will Cameron Smith, J.J. Spaun, Zach Johnson, or Akshay Bhatia — who, also standing on the 18th needing a number, also made a double bogey. The 18th hole on Friday collected more notable names than any single hole deserves to. Former champions Fred Couples, Jose Maria Olazabal, Vijay Singh, and Mike Weir all saw their Masters end at the halfway point, as Augusta concluded its business with its customary lack of sentimentality.

      🚨 Moving Day — Saturday Preview

      Fourteen consecutive Masters winners entered the weekend within four strokes of the lead. Not one player is within six of McIlroy heading into Saturday. The math is historically unusual. But Augusta National has its own relationship with history, and Amen Corner has swallowed leads that seemed far more comfortable than this one. Reed and Burns will attack the par-5s. Fleetwood, who has shown all week that he is playing some of the best golf of his life, will not go quietly. The question is not whether there will be drama on Saturday. There always is. The question is whether McIlroy can stay above it. So far, he has been the one creating it.

      📋 Pool Scoring Rules

      ⛳  Scoring

      Your top four golfers count throughout the tournament. Those four lowest cumulative scores make up your team total as the week unfolds.

      If a player misses the cut, that player is assigned +10 for each remaining round. In practical terms, that means +10 on Friday if needed and +20 across the weekend, so they are effectively out of contention for your counting four.

      🏁  Tiebreaker

      If there is a tie for first place, the tiebreaker is applied in this order:

      1) Whoever picked the Masters winner.

      2) The lower score from your 5th-best scorer.

      3) The lower score from your 6th-best scorer.

      4) If it is still tied, the pot is split 50/50.

      📸 Augusta Ace History

      Ross Somerville (long-lost relative of our tournament hosts, the Sommervilles) made the first hole-in-one in Masters history during the 1934 tournament, the very first year the event was played at Augusta National. The Canadian amateur aced the 16th hole, giving the Somerville name a permanent place in Augusta history from the start.

      Somerville was one of the leading amateurs of his era, and his ace came before the tournament was even officially known as the Masters Tournament.
