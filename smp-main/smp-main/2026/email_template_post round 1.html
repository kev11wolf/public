📅  Thursday, April 9, 2026  —  Augusta National Golf Club

Round One at Augusta — And Already, We Have Ourselves a Tournament.

 

Good evening, friends — and what a lovely first chapter it was at Augusta National. The opening round gave us a little bit of everything: some beautiful scoring, a few big roars, a couple of cruel turns, and a leaderboard that already feels both crowded and fragile at the same time.

As we head to Friday, Sam Burns and Rory McIlroy share the lead at 5-under, with Jason Day, Kurt Kitayama, and Patrick Reed just two back at 3-under. Right behind them is another dangerous group at 2-under: Shane Lowry, Justin Rose, Xander Schauffele, and Scottie Scheffler — close enough that one clean stretch tomorrow could turn this thing upside down in a hurry.

There were some memorable moments all across the grounds today. Burns came flying out with an eagle to set the tone for his round, Rory gathered real momentum on the inward half, and Patrick Reed gave the galleries something to talk about with two eagles on his first nine holes — the kind of start that gets everybody glancing at the board a little sooner than expected.

A few curve balls were mixed in as well. Shane Lowry holed out for eagle at 13, Adam Scott played Amen Corner in 3-under, and 60-year-old José María Olazábal looked like he had found a little fountain of youth with a 2-under 34 on his opening nine. On social and in the highlight clips, those were the shots making the rounds all afternoon.

And then, of course, Augusta reminded everyone that she keeps her own counsel. Bryson DeChambeau made a triple bogey at 11 after needing three shots to escape the bunker, and Fred Couples — who had worked his way to 2-under through 13 and briefly had the place dreaming — took a quadruple-bogey 9 at the 15th. That is how quickly the mood can change around here.

So who is in position going into Friday? Burns and McIlroy, obviously, have given themselves the best start, but this board still feels wonderfully unsettled. Day, Kitayama, Reed, Lowry, Rose, Xander, and Scheffler are all close enough to make Friday feel less like a second round and more like a fresh beginning.

Over in the pool, there is already a little daylight at the top. Paul W. leads the way at 7-under, with Evan S. one back at 6-under and Brandon sitting in a strong third position at 4-under. Just behind that, Nat S., Stefan R., Nate, Tommy, and Brady S. are all at 3-under, which means nobody near the top is sleeping easy tonight.

And for the home crowd update: K-Dawg is at 2-under, still very much in the mix with plenty of golf left and more than enough chaos still sitting out there on that back nine. One hot round tomorrow, one player catching fire, and the whole pool board can start to look very different.

A few things worth watching as Friday arrives:

🏆  At the top:  Burns and McIlroy have the first move, but there are too many proven names within two or three shots for anybody to get comfortable.

 

⚠️  Hard-luck stories:  DeChambeau’s bunker trouble and Couples’ late collapse were the day’s reminder that Augusta can turn on you in a heartbeat.

 

📊  Pool watch:  Paul W. has the overnight lead, Evan S. is right there, Brandon is lurking, and a whole cluster at 3-under is waiting for one Friday charge.

 

🏌  Friday feeling:  The men in front have position, but this still has the unmistakable feel of a Masters where the real sorting out is only just beginning.

Enjoy Friday at Augusta, keep one eye on the tournament board and the other on the pool standings, and settle in. The first round gave us plenty, but it also left just enough mystery in the air to make tomorrow feel very, very important.
