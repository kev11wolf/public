Round Three: Six Shots Gone, One Round Left, and Rory McIlroy Is on the Range at Dusk Trying to Find His Golf Swing.

 

Good evening, friends. Six shots. That is what Rory McIlroy walked into Saturday morning with — the largest 36-hole lead in the history of this tournament. By the time the Georgia pines went dark, every single one of those strokes was gone. Cameron Young, playing the round of his life, fired an eight-birdie 65. McIlroy, fighting what he later described as “a case of the lefts,” stumbled to a 73. They are tied at 11-under par heading into Sunday. Same score. One round left. The whole world watching. Augusta has a way of doing this.

The real damage happened at Amen Corner. McIlroy, who had looked untouchable through 36 holes, found trouble at the 11th and 12th as the wind picked up and the mood shifted. Young, meanwhile, was doing the opposite of falling apart — he birdied the 11th to pull to within two and never looked back. Sam Burns is one back at ‑10, Shane Lowry sits at ‑9, and Justin Rose and Jason Day are lurking at ‑8. After his round, McIlroy went straight to the range with his coach to work on keeping the ball out of the left rough. That image alone — Rory on the Augusta practice tee at dusk, fixing the thing that’s always been the thing — tells you everything about what Sunday means for him.

A few things worth knowing as we head into the final round:

🏆  The co-lead:  McIlroy and Young, heads-up, final round, Augusta National. Young has never won a major. McIlroy has been chasing a green jacket his entire career. This is what the sport was built for.

 

⛏  Amen Corner decides it:  Holes 11, 12, and 13 swallowed McIlroy’s lead on Saturday. Sunday’s champion will be whoever navigates that stretch with their nerve intact. Expect chaos.

 

⚡  Sam Burns lurking at ‑10:  One shot back, hungry, and overdue for something big. Burns has the game to go low at Augusta and he knows it. Don’t sleep on him.

 

🍀  The Irish angle:  Shane Lowry is two back at ‑9, walking the same fairways as Rory. If McIlroy wobbles, Lowry is right there ready to make it a very complicated Sunday for everyone involved.

 

🔧  Rory’s range session:  He was out there until dusk with Pete Cowen working on the driver. Either he fixed it or he didn’t. You will know by the third hole on Sunday, and so will he.

 

🏉  Jason Day at ‑8:  Three back, prior major champion, Augusta experience. Three shots on a Sunday at Augusta is nothing. Day is a problem nobody is talking about, which is exactly how he likes it.

Evan S. leads at ‑26 and is doing his best impression of the Rory McIlroy that existed before Saturday afternoon — out front, seemingly comfortable, and sitting on a two-shot lead over Paul W. Tommy is one shot behind Paul at ‑23, and Justin E. and Blake S. are right there at ‑22. There are five people within four strokes of the lead. This is not over.

And let’s be clear about what’s actually on the line here. The Sommerville Masters Pool green jacket. There is no Evan S. victory in this pool’s history. There is no Paul W. green jacket hanging in the closet. Sunday is a first for whoever takes it — and that cuts both ways. The pressure of being two shots clear with 18 holes of other people’s golf left to play is not nothing. Evan has earned the right to be nervous. Paul has earned the right to believe he can steal it. One of them could walk away from Sunday evening as the inaugural owner of their own piece of Sommerville Masters Pool lore. Or Tommy could ruin everything. That’s Augusta for you.

K-Dawg is at ‑19, six back — which in most pools is a concession speech. In this pool, with this group, it’s a spirited warm-up. Nate at ‑16 is the quiet one doing math nobody else is doing yet.
